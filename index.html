Why You Should Switch To Linux

by Farhan

Before discovering why you should switch to Linux, you need to understand what Linux is. Let's explore together why Linux stands out from other operating systems.

What Is Linux?

Linux is a free and open-source family of operating systems that is resilient and flexible. The system's source code is accessible to everyone, making it transparent how the system works. People from all across the world collaborate and continually develop Linux due to its openness.

Since its inception, Linux has grown into dependable and secure distributions used in countless devices, including PCs, phones, and supercomputers. Linux is cost-effective because it's free and lightweight, helping older machines stay functional longer.

Distributions (Distros)

You might be wondering what distros means...

A Linux distribution, or "distro," is a packaged version of Linux that includes the Linux kernel plus software and utilities that make the OS functional and user-friendly.

Some distros are optimized for business environments, offering enterprise tools, while others focus on multimedia with advanced editing software. Developer-focused distributions come preloaded with compilers, programming tools, and IDEs.

Why Should You Switch to Linux?

There are many compelling reasons to switch to Linux.

Lightweight

Modern Windows has become bloated with unnecessary software. Linux, on the other hand, is stripped of pre-installed garbage. You get a clean system without Candy Crush, random links, or excessive ads on a product you paid for.

Efficient

Linux is highly optimized and runs smoothly even on older hardware. It uses fewer system resources than Windows or macOS. Being modular, you can remove unwanted background services, making Linux faster, cleaner, and more efficient.

Gaming
